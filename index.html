Space Race!

The giant leap in space technology innovation of the 60s is a consequence of what is known as the "Space Race". The major players in the Cold War, the US and the USSR, displayed their technological capabilities by pushing the limits of what humans could do in space. From the first successful orbit (Sputnik in 1957) to the first successful Moon Landing (Apollo 11 in 1969), many feats of rocket design and space flight were accomplished. While a thinly veiled attempt to show off arms capabilities using technology and personell from Nazi research into V-2 Rocketry, such achievements in space flight represent one of the goods to come from US and USSR competition and have inspired multiple generations of scientists, engineers, and space explorers.

Join our very own Space Race today! Making progress in Kerbal Space Program can be tough, but being the first to accomplish something hard can be very rewarding. To help facilitate this, we will be having our own Space Race here. Check out the long list of possible accomplishments and try to be the first to accomplish some of them. We will record the date that you completed it and display the screenshot from the first successful mission. By the end, you should have your own library of accomplishments, and the class will have their collective achievements ready to show off!
